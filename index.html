Games Artists Play Spring 2020

We are going to start with dice and other chance operations, which are one of the most basic and defining characteristics of games. And are also fun. We'll start by looking some of the ways that artists have used chance to help make art.

On the first day of class, I'll be giving out some dice, and talking about some possible ways to use dice to make art.

Your job is to take them home and design a game-like system for making art, that we can use to generate art in class. If we will need any other materials, please bring those!

So for instance, we might roll some dice and draw the numbers onto a piece of paper.

Or we might assign colors or shapes to each die number and based on the dice rolls we might paint a shape or color.

Or we might roll the dice in paint and then throw them onto a canvas.

Cards!

In this unit we will look at cards, which are one of the basic building blocks of many games.

What are cards?

Cards are, often, a collection of images.

Cards are often a collection, or even multiple collections.

Cards are sometimes used to remember things.

Cards can be used for magic, or divination.

Cards are soemtimes the whole game and sometimes they are only part of the game.

We will look at a bunch of different types of cards in class, and we will make a card game!

Your cards can be works of art you've already made, collected and rearranged, or they can be new works of art.

Your game should have at least ten cards but 21-52 would be better!
